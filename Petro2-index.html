Description:

Sites where petrochemical compounds have been manufactured, distributed, transformed or spilled. Toxicity of sites varies, but almost all are likely to have hydrocarbon contamination. Common contaminants include PAHs, hydrocarbons, phenols, BTEX, arsenic, cyanide and lead. Cleanup of these sites is a complicated and expensive process. For some contaminants, bio-, phyto- or myco- remediation can be a viable method for removing or breaking down contaminants in the soil and groundwater, but for these methods timelines can stretch decades into the future.
